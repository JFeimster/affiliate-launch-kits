Approved Language

Use phrases that create clarity instead of liability confetti.

These phrases help Partners describe the process without promising outcomes. They should appear in scripts, handouts, resource pages, referral assets, and funding-related explanations.

Explore funding options

Frames the conversation as a review of possible paths, not a promised result.

May be available

Signals that options depend on review, business profile, and provider criteria.

Based on business profile

Connects possible options to the specific business owner’s actual situation.

Provider criteria apply

Makes clear that providers determine fit, requirements, terms, and availability.

Approval, terms, amounts, and timing are not guaranteed

Sets direct expectations around the limits of any funding-related conversation.

One application helps identify possible funding paths

Explains the purpose of application review without implying approval or qualification.

When traditional financing is delayed, unavailable, or not the right fit today

Useful referral framing for bankers, accountants, attorneys, and professional connectors.

Restricted Language

Do not publish these claims.

These phrases imply certainty, universal availability, or unsupported outcomes. They should not appear in Partner scripts, landing pages, handouts, social posts, emails, launch kits, or referral assets.

Guaranteed approval

Never guarantee provider decisions.

Everyone qualifies

Do not imply universal eligibility.

Credit does not matter

Do not dismiss provider review factors.

Same-day funding for everyone

Do not promise timing or availability.

Any amount available

Do not promise specific or unlimited amounts.

We approve what banks deny

Do not imply automatic approval after a bank decline.

No documents needed

Do not promise documentation requirements.

Instant funding guaranteed

Do not promise funding or speed.

Safe Rewrite Examples

Say the useful thing without lighting the claim on fire.

Most unsafe claims can be rewritten into exploratory, accurate language. The trick is to remove certainty and add the business profile/provider criteria frame.

Unsafe

Guaranteed approval for every business owner.

Safe Rewrite

Business owners can explore funding options that may be available based on their business profile. Provider criteria apply.

Unsafe

Get any amount you need today.

Safe Rewrite

Available options, amounts, terms, and timing depend on the business profile and provider criteria.

Unsafe

Credit does not matter.

Safe Rewrite

A business profile may be reviewed across multiple factors, and provider criteria apply.

Unsafe

We approve what banks deny.

Safe Rewrite

When traditional financing is delayed, unavailable, or not the right fit today, one application may help identify possible funding paths.

Unsafe

No documents needed and instant funding guaranteed.

Safe Rewrite

Documentation requirements, review timelines, and possible funding options vary by provider and business profile.

Partner Guidelines

Rules for Partner-facing funding language.

These rules apply to scripts, emails, handouts, referral assets, social posts, launch kits, resource pages, and internal enablement docs.

  • Use Partner as the umbrella term unless a resource is specifically about a defined lane.
  • Do not guarantee approval, funding amounts, rates, terms, timelines, qualification, or outcomes.
  • Use exploratory language when discussing funding options.
  • Explain that provider criteria apply.
  • Avoid implying that all business owners qualify.
  • Use the default disclaimer on funding-related resources.
  • Escalate uncertain claims for review before publishing.

Claim Review Checklist

Before publishing, run the claim through this filter.

If the copy cannot pass this checklist, it is not ready. The internet has enough financial goblin poetry already.

Approval and qualification

  • Does the copy avoid approval guarantees?
  • Does the copy avoid saying or implying everyone qualifies?
  • Does the copy avoid promising provider decisions?

Rates, terms, amounts, timing

  • Does the copy avoid specific rate promises?
  • Does the copy avoid specific term promises?
  • Does the copy avoid amount and timing guarantees?

Provider criteria

  • Does the copy explain that provider criteria apply?
  • Does the copy frame options as based on business profile?
  • Does the copy avoid making assumptions before review?

Safe framing

  • Does the copy frame the process as exploring possible funding paths?
  • Does the copy include or align with the default disclaimer?
  • Would a referral Partner feel comfortable standing behind it?

Final Rule

When in doubt, do not publish the claim.

Save the draft, rewrite it with exploratory language, and review it before it goes into a script, handout, email, launch kit, or social post. Clean copy compounds. Bad claims explode.
