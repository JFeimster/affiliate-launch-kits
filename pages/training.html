Training Paths

Six paths, one operating rhythm.

Training should move Partners from orientation to execution without overloading them with jargon, unsupported claims, or fifty-seven tabs of despair.

Partner Onboarding

Start with the OS structure, Partner lanes, resource hub, launch kits, and safe language.

Funding Basics

Understand how to discuss possible funding paths without promising approvals or outcomes.

Referral Partner Strategy

Learn how to approach bank managers, accountants, attorneys, dealers, and local connectors.

CRM & Tracking

Use lightweight trackers to manage activity, conversations, follow-up, and review cycles.

Follow-Up Rhythm

Build a weekly cadence for next steps, referral source check-ins, and business owner updates.

Responsible Funding Language

Use approved phrases and avoid guarantees around approval, terms, rates, amounts, timing, or qualification.

Recommended First Training Sequence

Do this before outreach.

Partners should complete the basics before sending referral emails, talking to business owners, or publishing funding-related copy.

  1. Read Start Here. Understand the basic operating path.
  2. Review Compliance. Learn approved and restricted language.
  3. Open Resources. Identify launch plans, scripts, trackers, and prompt packs.
  4. Study Referral Assets. Pick the correct audience lane.
  5. Use Trackers. Track daily actions and weekly reviews.

Training Disclaimer

Training resources help Partners communicate clearly and organize work. They do not guarantee funding, approvals, revenue, income, terms, rates, amounts, timing, or outcomes.
