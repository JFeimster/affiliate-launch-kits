First 7 Days

The Partner launch sprint.

The goal is not to become a funding oracle in a week. The goal is to understand the ecosystem, use compliant language, identify referral lanes, and start clean conversations.

1

Day 1: Orientation

Review the Partner Enablement OS structure, safe language, and current resource categories.

2

Day 2: Partner Lane

Clarify whether your main lane is referral outreach, funding conversations, operations, content, or channel development.

3

Day 3: Referral Map

List bank managers, accountants, attorneys, equipment dealers, and local connectors who already know business owners.

4

Day 4: Script Practice

Practice introducing the ecosystem as a way to explore funding options, not as a guaranteed approval machine.

5

Day 5: Daily 5

Begin tracking five useful Partner actions per day: intros, follow-ups, asset shares, reviews, or referral conversations.

6

Day 6: Review

Review what conversations happened, what assets were useful, and where follow-up is needed.

7

Day 7: Tighten

Refine your referral list, choose your next assets, and prepare the next week’s Partner action plan.

→

Daily Partner Actions

The Daily 5 keeps the machine moving.

Keep daily activity simple enough to execute and honest enough to measure. The tracker is about useful Partner actions, not vanity motion.

Daily 5 framework

Each day, choose five actions that move relationships, referrals, or follow-up forward. This can include one banker intro, one accountant follow-up, one asset share, one business owner conversation, and one CRM update.

Examples

  • Send one warm introduction
  • Follow up with one referral source
  • Share one compliant resource
  • Review one business owner conversation
  • Update one tracker or CRM record

What to Say

Use language that opens doors without writing checks your compliance cannot cash.

Partner conversations should be useful, clear, and careful. The safest posture is exploratory: the business owner can review possible funding paths based on business profile and provider criteria.

Safe opener

“Moonshine Capital helps business owners explore funding options that may be available based on their business profile.”

Referral source framing

“When traditional financing is delayed, unavailable, or not the right fit today, this can be a responsible next step to review possible options.”

Expectation setting

“Provider criteria apply, and approval, terms, amounts, and timing are not guaranteed.”

What Not to Say

Do not cosplay as the Approval Fairy.

Partners should never promise outcomes. Keep the conversation grounded in exploration, business profile, documentation, provider review, and clear expectations.

Avoid these claims

  • Guaranteed approval
  • Everyone qualifies
  • Credit does not matter
  • Same-day funding for everyone
  • Any amount available
  • Instant funding guaranteed

Use these instead

  • Explore funding options
  • May be available
  • Based on business profile
  • Provider criteria apply
  • One application helps identify possible funding paths
  • Approval, terms, amounts, and timing are not guaranteed
