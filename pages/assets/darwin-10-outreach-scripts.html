Darwin Hanneman — 10 Outreach Scripts

These scripts help Darwin start real conversations with referral partners and business owners without pressure or unsafe claims.

Compliance reminder: Funding options, approvals, terms, amounts, and timing are not guaranteed. Provider criteria apply and available options depend on the business profile.

Purpose

Create trust and move to a responsible next step.

Darwin helps business owners explore practical funding options through Moonshine Capital when banks are slow, traditional financing does not fit, or cash flow becomes the bottleneck.

The goal is to create trust, identify real funding needs, and move appropriate prospects toward a simple next step.

Core Positioning Line

“I help business owners explore practical funding options when traditional financing is delayed, unavailable, or not the right fit. One short application helps identify possible funding paths based on the business profile.”

Compliance Guardrails

Avoid saying or implying:

  • Everyone qualifies.
  • Approval is guaranteed.
  • Funding is guaranteed.
  • You’ll get funded today.
  • Credit does not matter.
  • We approve what banks deny.
  • Any business can get any amount.
  • This is free money.
  • The bank failed you.

Safer Language

  • Explore funding options.
  • May be available.
  • Based on business profile.
  • Provider criteria apply.
  • Approval, funding amount, terms, and timing are not guaranteed.
  • The application helps identify possible funding paths.

1. Bank Manager Walk-In Script

Use when Darwin walks into a local bank branch or meets a banker in person.

“I’m not here to compete with the bank. I’m looking to be a resource for business clients you cannot help right now — the ones who may be solid operators but do not fit the bank’s box today because of timing, collateral, credit profile, documentation, or underwriting limits.”

Soft CTA: What kind of business clients do you most often have to turn away or delay?

2. Bank Manager Email

Use to introduce Darwin as a commercial funding Partner with Moonshine Capital.

Key angle: when a business client is declined, delayed, underqualified, short on collateral, or outside bank criteria, Darwin may help them explore alternative funding options through one short application.

Include the disclaimer that approval, terms, funding amount, and timing are not guaranteed and depend on business profile and provider criteria.

3. Bank Manager Follow-Up

Use after a banker conversation, email, voicemail, or in-person visit.

“The easiest way to think of me is as a next-step resource for business clients who may not fit traditional bank financing right now.”

Ask whether it would be useful to send a short referral guide.

4. Accountant Intro Email

Use for accountants and bookkeepers who see cash-flow pressure before lenders do.

Common situations include payroll pressure, tax obligations, equipment needs, expansion costs, inventory or materials, slow receivables, and working capital gaps.

5. Attorney Intro Email

Use for attorneys who work with clients forming, buying, expanding, or transitioning businesses.

Darwin should clarify that he does not provide legal, tax, or accounting advice and that funding is not guaranteed.

6. Equipment Dealer Walk-In Script

Use with equipment dealers, repair shops, or vendors when funding may be the bottleneck for a customer.

Good use cases include equipment purchases, repairs, working capital, payroll support, job-start capital, and keeping revenue-producing equipment moving.

7. Trucking Facebook Group Post

Use to ask trucking owners about repair, equipment, payroll, fuel, slow-paying customers, and funding bottlenecks.

Keep it clear: no guaranteed approvals, no universal qualification, and no magic-money pitch.

8. Construction LinkedIn Message

Use for construction and contractor professionals dealing with materials, payroll, equipment, job mobilization, delayed receivables, or bank timing.

“If funding ever becomes the bottleneck on a job, I’m happy to be a resource.”

9. Referral Thank-You Message

Use when someone sends Darwin a referral or makes an introduction.

Set expectations: professional follow-up, clear process, no pressure, and no wild promises.

10. Denied-by-Bank Client Rescue Message

Use when a business owner has been delayed or declined by a bank and needs a responsible next step.

Position the application as a way to explore possible funding paths based on the business profile and provider criteria.
