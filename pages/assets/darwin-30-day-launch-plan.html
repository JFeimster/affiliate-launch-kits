Darwin Hanneman 30-Day Funding Partner Launch Plan

Mission

Start real conversations.

Darwin’s first 30 days are not about building a giant brand or trying to explain every product. The mission is to start real conversations with referral partners who already know business owners with funding problems.

Darwin should operate as a relationship-first commercial funding connector backed by Moonshine Capital’s broader funding ecosystem.

Positioning Statement

Darwin helps business owners explore commercial funding options through one simple application, backed by Moonshine Capital’s broader funding partner network.

W1

Week 1 — Setup, Positioning, and First Conversations

Objective: Get Darwin’s basic launch assets ready and begin low-pressure outreach.

  • Finalize public-facing identity.
  • Confirm application link and QR code.
  • Build first referral target list.
  • Start warm conversations.

Week 1 win condition

Working application link/QR code, a starter list of 50 referral prospects, and at least 10 real conversations started.

W2

Week 2 — Bank, Accountant, and Attorney Referral Push

Objective: Start building B2B referral lanes that can produce repeat lead flow.

  • Bank manager outreach.
  • Accountant/bookkeeper outreach.
  • Attorney outreach.
  • First referral one-pager testing.

Week 2 win condition

Identify one serious referral partner candidate and submit 2–3 total applications from early conversations.

W3

Week 3 — Niche Validation and Application Flow

Objective: Find out which lane produces conversations and applications.

  • Track which referral source responds best.
  • Push the strongest lane harder.
  • Improve follow-up language.
  • Review early application quality.

Week 3 win condition

Clear sense of which 1–2 referral lanes deserve more attention and 5–10 total applications submitted or in motion.

W4

Week 4 — Review, Tighten, and Scale the Winning Lane

Objective: Turn scattered outreach into a repeatable weekly operating rhythm.

  • Review metrics.
  • Identify best referral source.
  • Improve messaging.
  • Set Month 2 plan.

Week 4 win condition

Repeatable weekly rhythm, top-performing referral lane, and 3–5 referral partners worth nurturing.

What to Avoid

  • Do not say everyone qualifies.
  • Do not promise same-day funding as a universal outcome.
  • Do not make the bank look bad.
  • Do not say “we approve what banks deny.”
  • Do not confuse activity with progress.
  • Do not add five new niches before validating one.

Safe Talk Tracks

“I help business owners explore practical funding options when traditional financing is delayed, unavailable, or not the right fit today. One short application helps identify possible funding paths based on the business profile.”
“The first step is not a hard pitch. It’s getting enough basic information to see what funding paths may fit. If nothing makes sense, we say that.”
“If funding is holding up a repair, equipment purchase, payroll, materials, or a job start, the short application can help identify what options may be available based on business profile.”
