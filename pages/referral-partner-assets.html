Referral Audiences

Different referral sources, different conversation angles.

The audience decides the asset. A bank manager needs a different handout than an equipment dealer. An accountant needs different framing than a trucking company owner. Precision beats spray-and-pray marketing sludge.

Bank Managers

For clients who may need another route when bank financing is delayed, unavailable, or not the right fit today.

Accountants

For business owners facing cash-flow timing, tax-season pressure, growth needs, or working capital questions.

Attorneys

For business clients navigating transactions, disputes, expansion, or operational pressure.

Equipment Dealers

For businesses exploring equipment, expansion, replacement, repair, or operational capital needs.

Trucking Businesses

For operators with equipment, fuel, maintenance, insurance, receivables, or growth-related capital needs.

Construction Businesses

For contractors managing materials, payroll, mobilization costs, seasonal gaps, or project timing issues.

Local Connectors

For community operators who know business owners before the internet does. Old-school signal, new-school system.

Business Bankers

For bankers who want to preserve the relationship while giving a business owner a responsible next step.

Bank Manager Asset Section

When the bank cannot help directly.

The bank manager conversation should protect trust. The goal is not to position Moonshine Capital as a magic replacement for bank financing. The goal is to provide a responsible next step when traditional financing is delayed, unavailable, or not the right fit today.

  • Use a simple referral handout.
  • Explain that one application helps identify possible funding paths.
  • Clarify that provider criteria apply.
  • Avoid saying or implying that approval is guaranteed.

Safe Positioning

“When traditional financing is delayed, unavailable, or not the right fit today, this can give the business owner a responsible way to explore funding options based on their business profile.”

Accountants & Attorneys

Professional advisors need clean framing.

Accountants and attorneys tend to care about trust, precision, timing, and risk. Do not hand them hype. Hand them a clear explanation they can stand behind.

Accountant angle

“Some business owners need to explore funding options around cash-flow timing, seasonal pressure, receivables, or growth. Available options depend on business profile and provider criteria.”

Attorney angle

“When a business client needs to review possible funding paths connected to operations, transactions, or timing pressure, this can be a responsible referral option. Approval and terms are not guaranteed.”

Equipment, Trucking & Construction

Asset-heavy businesses often have timing problems.

These businesses may need to explore funding options around equipment, repairs, payroll, receivables, insurance, materials, fuel, or project cycles. Keep the conversation specific, useful, and clean.

Equipment Dealers

Useful when buyers need to review possible funding paths for equipment-related needs or working capital.

Trucking Businesses

Useful for conversations around trucks, repairs, fuel, receivables, insurance, and cash-flow timing.

Construction Businesses

Useful for project timing, payroll, materials, mobilization, retainage, and seasonal pressure.

Compliance-Safe Positioning

Referral assets should invite review, not promise outcomes.

  • Explore funding options
  • May be available
  • Based on business profile
  • Provider criteria apply
  • One application helps identify possible funding paths
  • Approval, terms, amounts, and timing are not guaranteed

Keep Out

Do not put these claims in referral assets.

  • Guaranteed approval
  • Everyone qualifies
  • Credit does not matter
  • Same-day funding for everyone
  • Any amount available
  • Instant funding guaranteed
