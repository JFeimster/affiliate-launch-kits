# Three Common Client Situations

## The Client Is Solid. The File Isn’t Bankable Yet.

The business may have revenue, customers, contracts, equipment needs, and a real business problem — but the file does not fit today because of collateral, credit, time in business, documentation, cash-flow timing, or underwriting policy.

**Best capital angle:** Fast Working Capital, Flex Revenue Advance, Business Line Access, Equipment Financing, or Larger Funding Reviews.

## The Opportunity Cannot Wait for a Slow Approval Cycle.

The client needs payroll, materials, inventory, equipment, repairs, or job-start capital before a traditional process can move. Slow money can cost the client the job.

**Best capital angle:** Fast Working Capital, Business Line Access, Invoice & Receivables Funding, or Equipment & Truck Financing.

## The Client May Become Bankable Later — If They Survive the Gap Now.

Some business owners need a bridge, a cleanup period, stronger revenue history, better documentation, or capital to keep operations moving while the business matures.

**Best capital angle:** Business Line Access, Structured Growth Funding, Equipment Financing, Startup Funding Path, or Larger Funding Reviews.
